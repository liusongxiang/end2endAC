Audio Samples from "End-to-End Accent Conversion"

Authors: Songxiang Liu, Disong Wang, Yuewen Cao, Lifa Sun, Xixin Wu, Shiyin Kang, Xunying Liu and Helen Meng

Abstract: Techniques for accent conversion (AC) aim to convert non-native-accented speech to native-accented one, without changing the voice. Previous AC methods require native-accented reference utterances during accent conversion, which hinders their practical usage in real-world applications. In this paper, we present an end-to-end framework for AC. An accented automatic speech recognition (ASR) model is used to extract linguistic representations from non-native-accented utterances. A speaker encoder is trained using a speaker-discriminative loss to generate speaker representation of a non-native-accented speaker. A multi-speaker text-to-speech (TTS) synthesis model is adopted to generate converted speech from the linguistic representations, conditioned on the speaker representation. Experimental results show that the proposed system can convert Hindi-accented English speech into accent-free one with high speech naturalness, which is indistinguishable from native speech in accent.
